🟊 THIS SUBMISSION WAS PART OF ISSUE 1, "THINGS I CAN'T GOOGLE" 🟊

Written by |

“Why are you trying so hard to fit in when you were born to stand out?” - Ian Wallace

Looking for the magic list? Maybe you were expecting to find here a series of steps to discover yourself, shine, have style and be authentic. Sorry, this isn’t google. But I get it. I’ve Googled “how to be myself” too, like there’s some secret formula.

There isn’t.

Learning to be yourself is more about getting lost, stumbling, questioning everything, and somehow enjoying the process as you figure out what “being you” actually means, without losing your essence in the process.

Social media is full of guides and trends that promise to help you become your best self. But sometimes, they can take you further away from who you really are. It’s super common to find posts like: “How to dress for your body type”, “How to gain confidence in 5 days”, “How to be aesthetic”, or “How to be the coolest person in class.”

Everything seems to have a formula. And if you don’t fit into those formulas… you feel like you’re doing something wrong. Like you’re failing at the most basic thing: being yourself.

Being yourself isn't always clear or easy. Some days you’ll feel complete, unstoppable. Other days you’ll feel confused, out of place, even scared. And that’s okay. It’s actually the most real thing that can happen. What matters is not living your life trying to squeeze into lists that don’t define you.

The truth is, there are no three steps to being yourself. Sometimes there are a thousand, sometimes none, and no matter how hard you search on Google, you won’t find the answer there. You find it while living, during a conversation with your friends, laughing with your family, or just doing something you love alone, without thinking of anything else.

The internet might give you ideas, advice, even pretty quotes. But it won’t teach you what to do when none of those lists work. It won’t tell you how to have a bad day and still choose yourself, or how to mess up and not let that define you.

And it definitely won’t prepare you for that moment when you look at yourself in the mirror, no filter, no posing, not trying to be someone else, and still, somehow, manage to say: “Yeah. This is me. With my mistakes, my weird parts, my good days and bad ones. But it’s me. And that’s enough.”

So if you made it this far looking for a quick guide, I’ll leave you with a classic from someone who knew how to roll with the punches:

“Make the plan. Execute the plan. Expect the plan to go off the rails. Throw away the plan.” — Leonard Snart

Because in the end, being yourself isn’t a step, or a box to check. It’s everything you do when the plan falls apart… and you still decide to keep going.
