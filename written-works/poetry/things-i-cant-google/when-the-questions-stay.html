🟊 THIS SUBMISSION WAS PART OF ISSUE 1, "THINGS I CAN'T GOOGLE" 🟊

Written by |

Image by Penny from Pixabay

I can ask how far the moon is,
how deep the Mariana trench runs,
how long it takes a broken bone to mend—
but I can’t ask why
I still flinch
when kindness walks in.

Search engines don’t do
the midnight version of me:
barefoot on the kitchen tile,
palms pressed to a silence
too thick to swallow.
No autocomplete for
how do you know if you’re healing right?
how much absence turns a person into a myth?

I can look up
every stage of grief,
but none of them mention
the way joy makes me guilty
or why I reread texts
from people I no longer speak to
like they hold the last trace
of who I used to be.

Maps can find the route
to the city I left,
but not the version of myself
that used to belong there.
Not the way home
when the house still stands
but nothing inside
remembers me.

I can learn the science
of echo and orbit,
but not
what it means when someone leaves
and still lives in your habits.
Not how to let go
without feeling like
you’re unpeeling
your own skin.

There is no URL
for the way some memories
arrive with both a smile
and a bruise.
No FAQ for
how to love again
when the last time
burned the blueprint.

Some answers
refuse the click.
Some truths
only come
when you stop
looking..
