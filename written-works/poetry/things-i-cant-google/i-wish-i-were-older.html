🟊 THIS SUBMISSION WAS PART OF ISSUE 1, "THINGS I CAN'T GOOGLE" 🟊

Written by |

I said, “I wish I were older”, but it went
From crying over a scraped knee,
And a push on the playground,
To crying over texts left unseen,
And the people I can never say a last goodbye to.

I said, “I wish I were older”, but it changed
From being excited about growing,
The excitement of being in control,
And the freedom of moving out,
To longing for the comforts of a true “home”,
And a warmth that I can now only dream about.

I said “I wish I were older”, but she disappeared,
The child who once asked a million questions
About everything in and out of sight,
To a grown-up, ignorant of the unknown,
Stuck in a loop that drains them of their might.

I said, “I wish I were older”, because
growing up seemed enticing: doing something 24/7 and infinite possibilities,
But it’s all a scam: being a workaholic does more harm than good,
And infinite choice is a euphemism for “liabilities”.

Now I say, “I wish I were younger”, because
I just want to scribble on paper,
Without worrying about judgement,
And without a care in the world, because
This fear of social exile is just another ailment.

Now I say, “I wish I were younger”, because
I want the clock to rewind, to show me
How I could’ve spent my time,
Skip around in the playground, or
Play pretend with toys,
I just want to relive a life without this permanent frown.

Now I say, “I wish I were younger”,
because I want to laugh at the silliest of things,
Not pretend I’m fine when I’m clearly not.
I wish I had never wished at all.
